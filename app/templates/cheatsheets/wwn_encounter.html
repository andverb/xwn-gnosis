Reaction Rolls

2d6 + Cha mod (if PCs can greet)

Roll for every encounter unless bloodshed is inevitable. Clue PCs into results before they act.

2- Hostile as the situation allows
3-5 More unfriendly than expected
6-8 Predictably hostile or friendly
9-11 More friendly than expected
12+ As friendly and helpful as possible

Peaceful Reactions

What "friendly" means for hostile groups:

  • Demand a bribe - Money, gear, food, praise; haggling possible
  • Back off - Keep distance, let groups pass; may report later
  • Ask for favors - Enemy killed, task done; offer to ignore PCs in return
  • Offer tribute - Wealth, info, services to be left alone
  • Willingly socialize - Lonely, mistaken identity, or just chatty

Remember: Reaction rolls are just the start. Clever words, gifts, or insults can shift attitudes.

Surprise & Initiative

Surprise

If GM decides surprise is possible, roll Wis/Notice vs Dex/Sneak. If attackers win, they act full round before initiative is rolled.

Ambush might provoke Morale check from unmilitary or undisciplined targets (GM discretion).

Initiative

1d8 + highest Dex (or Int) mod

Each side rolls. Winning side acts first in any order, then the other. NPCs usually have no modifier unless quick. PCs win ties.

Variant: Each participant rolls their own initiative and acts in order.

Fleeing & Escape

If foes pursue (many don't), escapee with best Dex/Exert rolls vs fastest pursuer. Success = ~1 turn to safety.

Escapees outnumber pursuers +1
Good escape plan +1
Slowed by slowest member -2

Morale Checks

2d6 > ML = Fail

On failure: NPC retreats, surrenders, or seeks to escape.

When to Roll

  • First casualty - When first member of a group is killed or incapacitated
  • Losing badly - Group starts to visibly lose or odds of victory shrink
  • Terror - Facing terrifying magic, horrendous slaughter, or vastly superior foe
  • Non-combatant - Civilian first faced with serious physical harm
  • Ambush - Undisciplined targets may check when ambushed (GM discretion)

Failure Consequences

  • Non-combatants: Flee madly, drop everything
  • Trained fighters: Fighting withdrawal, try to escape with comrades
  • If escape impossible: Surrender, collapse in despair, or fight with frenzy

Typical ML: 2-5 cowardly, 6-7 average, 8-9 brave, 10-11 fearless, 12 unbreakable. PCs never check.

Instinct Checks

1d10 ≤ Inst = Fail

On failure: NPC acts impulsively, short-sightedly, instinctually.

When to Roll

  • Round 2+ - Second round of combat for mobs and undisciplined fighters
  • After Morale - Creature just had to make a Morale check
  • Confusion - Enemy did something confusing or disorienting
  • Provoked - Enemy enraged or directly intimidated the creature
  • Temptation - Presented with something it desires (food, money, etc.)

Failure Examples

  • Attack sub-optimal targets blindly
  • Use unarmed attacks instead of weapon in hand
  • Aim spells/shots at targets of lesser importance
  • Chase after dropped bait instead of fighting

Typical Inst: 5+ beasts/civilians, 3-4 veterans, 1-2 hardened, 0 coldest killers. PCs never check. Optional.

Running NPCs & Fleeing

NPC Combat Tips

  • NPCs won't fight to the death unless they must
  • Check Morale when things go badly
  • Check Instinct when confused or provoked
  • Let NPCs make tactical errors - they're not perfect
  • Once Morale fails, side won't fight those foes until courage recovered

GM Guidance

  • Be charitable about letting PCs run away
  • Most pursuers won't be relentlessly persistent
  • Running away is a viable option, not an excuse to die tired

Chases & Pursuit

Foot Chase

  1. Fleeing pace: Best Dex/Exert or Con/Exert in fleeing group rolls = Pace
  2. Helpers: Other fleeing members hinder pursuit (skill check or GM judgment). Success = +1 to Pace each, max +3
  3. Pursuers roll: Single Dex/Exert or Con/Exert check with modifiers
  4. Result: Beat Pace = caught. Tie or less = escaped

Foot Chase Pace Modifiers

No head start at all +2
One round head start +1
<1 minute head start +0
>1 minute head start -2
More pursuers than pursued -1
Terrain knowledge -2 to +2
Half-hearted / Enraged -1 / +1

Mounted Chase

  1. Fleeing pace: Fleeing rider rolls Dex/Ride = Pace
  2. Each pursuer rolls: Dex/Ride with modifiers
  3. Result: Meet or exceed Pace = catch up. Fail = fall behind & lost

Note: Unmounted pursuers cannot catch mounted evaders in short-term chases.

Mounted Chase Pace Modifiers

Can't directly see pursued -2
Pursuer flying, pursued not +3
Pursued flying, pursuer not -3
Spotter relaying position +1
Terrain knowledge -2 to +2
Half-hearted / Enraged -1 / +1
